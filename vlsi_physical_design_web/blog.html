What You Will Find in This Blog

This blog section is intended for practical VLSI physical design notes that extend the concept pages. Instead of repeating textbook definitions, posts can focus on real engineering decisions such as floorplan tradeoffs, congestion cleanup strategies, timing closure iterations, power planning choices, and signoff debugging patterns.

A useful blog post in this domain usually answers three questions: what problem appeared, how it was diagnosed, and which changes solved it. That structure makes the content more valuable for readers searching specific physical design issues and helps build topical authority for the site.

Suggested Post Categories

To improve search coverage and reader navigation, future posts can be grouped into recurring categories such as STA debugging, placement and congestion optimization, CTS tuning, ECO workflows, low-power implementation, and physical verification signoff checks.

  • Interview-style explainers for common PD questions and terminology
  • Tool workflow breakdowns with inputs, reports, and expected outputs
  • Case studies showing timing, power, and area tradeoffs
  • Checklist posts for tapeout readiness and signoff review meetings

Publishing even short but detailed posts in these categories will strengthen SEO and make the blog a practical resource for backend engineers.

As new articles are added, link them back to the core concept pages so readers can move between fundamentals and applied examples. This improves usability for students and engineers, and it also helps search engines understand the topical relationship between your physical design tutorials, troubleshooting notes, and project case studies.
